## 0. The one idea behind this whole plan

You said it best: "the website itself is not much use to me." You are completely right, and that is the right instinct.

A website does not generate patients. It is the storefront. It is where people land, get convinced, and put their hand up. On its own it just sits there.

What generates patients is a **system** wrapped around that storefront:

> the right Australians see us, click, land on a page built to convince them, qualify themselves through a smart form, become a lead, get contacted fast while they are still warm, get nurtured until they are ready, and book a consultation that turns into a deposit.

The website you have approved is one link in that chain. This document is the rest of the chain. Build all of it and the site stops being a brochure and starts being a machine that turns advertising dollars into booked surgeries.

The rest of this playbook is that machine, broken into parts you can fund and switch on in order.

## 1. The funnel, end to end

Think of it as a series of gates. Out of everyone who sees us, a fraction click, a fraction of those become leads, a fraction of those book. Our job is to widen every gate.

| Stage | What happens | Where | Healthy benchmark |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Reach | An Australian sees BBDV | Google Search, Instagram, Facebook, YouTube | n/a |
| 2. Click | They click through | Ad or post | 3 to 8% of searches, 0.8 to 2% of social impressions |
| 3. Land | They arrive on a page built for that exact intent | Dedicated landing page (not the homepage) | n/a |
| 4. Qualify | They tell us procedure, budget, timeframe, location | Multi-step quote form | 4 to 8% of landing page visitors become leads |
| 5. Lead | We capture name, contact, preferred channel | Form submit, WhatsApp, call | n/a |
| 6. Contact | We reach them fast, ideally within minutes | WhatsApp, phone, email | aim for first contact under 1 hour |
| 7. Consult | They book a free online consultation with a surgeon | Calendar booking | 30 to 40% of qualified leads |
| 8. Deposit | They commit and pay a booking deposit | Invoice, EMI option | 15 to 25% of consults |
| 9. Surgery | They travel and have the procedure | Bangalore | n/a |
| 10. Advocate | They review, refer, and appear in patient stories | Google, Instagram, site | compounding free growth |

The numbers in the right column are the levers. A site that lifts stage 4 from 4% to 7%, and a follow-up process that lifts stage 6 contact speed, together can roughly **double** booked patients from the **same** ad spend. That is why the funnel matters more than the ad budget.

### A worked example (starter budget)

Assume A$3,000 per month in ads, blended across Google and Meta. Conservative mid-range numbers:

- Reach and clicks: roughly 350 to 450 qualified visitors land on our pages.
- Lead conversion at 5%: about **20 to 30 qualified leads per month**.
- Cost per qualified lead: roughly **A$100 to A$150**.
- Leads to consultations at 35%: about **8 to 10 consultations**.
- Consultations to deposits at 20%: about **2 to 3 booked patients per month**.

Now compare that to the value of a booked patient. A cosmetic surgery holiday is a multi-thousand-dollar decision. Even a modest facilitation margin per booked patient makes a customer acquisition cost of a few hundred dollars per booking extremely healthy. We will fill your real margin into this model together, but in almost every realistic scenario the math works, and it improves every month as the funnel is tuned.

## 2. The economics, in plain numbers

The reason cosmetic medical tourism advertising works is the gap between Australian prices and the all-in cost in India. That gap is your entire sales argument, and it is real.

| Procedure | Typical Australia price | BBDV from price (Bangalore) | Saving |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rhinoplasty | ~A$15,000 | from ~A$4,200 | ~70% |
| Facelift | ~A$30,000 | from ~A$6,500 | ~78% |
| Breast augmentation | ~A$12,000 to A$20,000 | indicative, confirmed on consult | major |
| Liposuction | ~A$5,000 to A$12,000 | indicative | major |
| Tummy tuck | ~A$12,000 to A$20,000 | indicative | major |

(Prices already in the site content layer. All marked indicative and confirmed after a personal consultation, which keeps us honest and compliant.)

What this means for advertising: even a generous cost to acquire one booked patient is a small fraction of the value of that booking. That is the definition of an advertising channel worth scaling. The job is simply to **not waste** the spend, which is what the funnel, the landing pages, and the speed of follow-up are for.

### The metrics that decide profitability

- **CPL, cost per qualified lead.** Target A$80 to A$150 in the AU cosmetic niche. Driven by ad quality and landing page conversion rate.
- **CAC, cost to acquire one booked patient.** CPL divided by your lead-to-booking rate. Target well under your margin per booking.
- **ROAS, return on ad spend.** Revenue from bookings divided by ad spend. We are aiming for a multiple, not a fraction.
- **Speed to lead.** The single most overlooked lever. Contacting a lead in 5 minutes versus 5 hours can multiply booking rates several times over. More on this in section 6.

## 3. Channel strategy

We do not spend everywhere at once. We start with the channel where the intent is highest and the buyer is closest to deciding, then expand. That channel is Google Search.

### 3.1 Google Search ads (primary, highest intent)

When an Australian types "rhinoplasty cost overseas" or "cosmetic surgery India package," they are already shopping. We want to be there with a message-matched ad and a landing page that answers exactly that search.

**Campaign structure (clean and accountable):**

- Campaign A, Procedure intent. Ad groups per procedure: Rhinoplasty, Breast Augmentation, Liposuction, Facelift, Tummy Tuck, Gynecomastia, Hair Transplant. Each ad group points to that procedure's dedicated landing page.
- Campaign B, Destination and savings intent. Ad groups around "cosmetic surgery India," "surgery holiday," "cosmetic surgery overseas," "save on cosmetic surgery."
- Campaign C, Competitor and alternative intent. People comparing Thailand and Bali (the obvious competitors) who can be shown why Bangalore and Aesthetics Plus is the safer, board-certified choice.
- Campaign D, Brand. Cheap protection so competitors do not bid on "BBDV" and "Body By Design Vacations."

**Three intent tiers of keywords:**

1. High intent, buy soon: "rhinoplasty india price," "breast augmentation overseas package," "cosmetic surgery bangalore cost." Fewer searches, highest conversion, worth the most.
2. Research intent: "is cosmetic surgery in india safe," "cosmetic surgery india vs thailand," "best country for plastic surgery." Capture early, nurture.
3. Local price-shock intent: "rhinoplasty cost australia," "facelift price melbourne." People reacting to Australian prices, ripe for the savings message.

**Negative keywords (stop wasting money) from day one:** "jobs," "training," "surgeon salary," "free," "courses," "how to become," "near me" when irrelevant, plus low-budget procedure terms that do not fit. We refine this list weekly.

**Budget approach:** start narrow on the highest-intent procedures (rhinoplasty, breast, lipo, gynecomastia convert well in this niche), prove cost per lead, then widen. Do not spread A$3,000 across 20 procedures. Concentrate, learn, expand.

**The compliance reality (important and handled honestly).** Google has specific policies for healthcare and cosmetic advertising, and Australia has its own rules through AHPRA and the TGA about how cosmetic surgery can be advertised. We respect both. In practice that means: no guarantees of results, no scaremongering, no before-and-after used as a promise, clear "results vary" and "indicative price" language, and positioning BBDV as a coordination and facilitation service partnered with a board-certified clinic. The site already uses this exact language. This keeps the account approved and the brand trustworthy. See section 9.

### 3.2 Meta ads, Facebook and Instagram (you already have the pages)

Google captures people already searching. Meta creates demand among people who were not searching yet but fit the profile. You are already running regional pages, so this is an extension, not a cold start.

- **Cold prospecting:** target Australian women and men in the cosmetic-interest profile with strong creative (real patient stories, the savings angle, the recovery-holiday angle). Drive to landing pages, not just the page.
- **Lead-form vs landing page:** Meta instant lead forms are cheap but lower quality. We will test instant forms for volume against landing-page traffic for quality, and let the booking rate decide, not the cost per lead.
- **Retargeting (the highest ROI spend on Meta):** anyone who visited the site, watched a video, or started the quote form but did not finish gets shown follow-up ads. This is cheap and converts because they already know us.
- **Lookalike audiences:** once we have a few hundred real leads, Meta can find more people who resemble them. This gets more efficient over time.
- **Creative angles that work in this niche:** transformation and confidence, the price gap shown honestly, "board-certified surgeons, 20,000+ patients since 2003," the recovery-as-a-getaway story, and short surgeon-credibility clips.

### 3.3 YouTube

You have seven real Aesthetics Plus videos already. YouTube ads (and the videos embedded on the site) build trust faster than text ever can, because the buyer can see the clinic and hear the surgeons. Low-cost in-stream ads targeted at cosmetic and travel interests, plus retargeting site visitors, are a strong stage-two channel once Search and Meta are stable.

### 3.4 SEO and content (the compounding, near-free channel)

Paid ads stop the moment you stop paying. SEO compounds. The site is already built for it (clean structure, per-procedure pages, sitemap, fast Next.js). On top of that we publish genuinely useful content that ranks and pulls in free traffic month after month:

- "How much does rhinoplasty cost in Australia vs India" comparison guides.
- "Is cosmetic surgery in India safe" trust and accreditation explainers.
- "What a cosmetic surgery holiday in Bangalore actually looks like" itinerary content.
- Patient story long-reads using the real testimonials.

This is a 3 to 6 month payoff, started early so it is paying off by the time ads alone get expensive.

### 3.5 The glue: retargeting everywhere

Most people do not convert on the first visit for a decision this big. A retargeting layer across Google Display, YouTube, and Meta keeps BBDV in front of everyone who showed interest, at a fraction of the cost of finding them the first time. This alone often lifts total conversions 20 to 40%.

## 4. Landing pages (why not just the homepage)

This is the single biggest leak in most medical-tourism funnels, and the easiest to fix.

**Sending paid traffic to the homepage wastes it.** Someone who searched "breast augmentation India price" and lands on a general homepage has to hunt for what they wanted. Many leave. A dedicated landing page that says exactly what they searched, immediately, converts two to three times better.

**Anatomy of a high-converting BBDV landing page:**

1. Headline that matches the ad and the search ("Breast Augmentation in India, from a fraction of the Australian price").
2. The savings, shown clearly and marked indicative, with a single primary action: get my quote.
3. Trust, fast and high: board-certified surgeons, 20,000+ patients since 2003, the Karnataka award, the partner clinic, real reviews.
4. The quote form, embedded high on the page, not buried at the bottom.
5. Real patient stories and before-and-after handled compliantly.
6. The recovery-holiday story (what the trip looks like, support, coordination from Melbourne).
7. FAQ that removes the specific fears (safety, surgeons, what if something goes wrong, how payment works, EMI).
8. WhatsApp and call options visible the entire way down.

**Which landing pages to build first** (matched to the first ad campaigns): Rhinoplasty, Breast Augmentation, Liposuction, Gynecomastia, Hair Transplant, plus one "Cosmetic Surgery in India, the safe and affordable way" general savings page for Campaign B. These are templated, so once one exists the rest are fast.

## 5. The qualifying form and lead scoring

The form is where a visitor becomes a lead, and where we decide how much that lead is worth before we have even spoken to them. The current multi-step form already captures the right things: procedures of interest, country and state, timeframe, budget, contact details, preferred contact method, and how they heard about us. That is genuinely strong and already ahead of many competitors.

We turn it into a sales weapon by **scoring** each lead automatically from what they tell us:

- **Hot:** timeframe within 3 months, budget stated, specific procedure, gave a phone number and prefers WhatsApp or call. Contact within minutes.
- **Warm:** 3 to 6 months, exploring budget, clear procedure. Contact same day, then nurture.
- **Cool:** "just researching," no timeframe, no budget. Drop into the email and content nurture, no hard sell yet.

This tells Shree and the coordinators exactly who to call first, so the limited human time goes where the money is. It is a small addition to the form data and a big lift to efficiency.

## 6. Speed to lead and follow-up (where most money is actually lost)

You can do everything above perfectly and still lose most of it here. This is the part competitors are worst at, which makes it your biggest opportunity.

**The 5-minute rule.** Research across lead industries is brutally consistent: contacting a new lead within 5 minutes versus even an hour can multiply the chance of converting them several times over. People fill in forms for several providers at once. The first human to respond, warmly and usefully, usually wins the patient. So:

- Every lead triggers an **instant** WhatsApp and email auto-response so they know they are heard, then a **fast human** follow-up.
- Hot leads get a real WhatsApp message or call as fast as humanly possible during AU hours.

**The Australia to India timezone reality (turn it into an advantage).** Bangalore is behind Melbourne, but the clinic answers email and WhatsApp 11am to 5pm their time. We design the follow-up so an AU lead is always answered promptly by the Melbourne front line (Shree), with the clinic looped in for clinical questions and consultation scheduling. The lead never feels a gap.

**WhatsApp as the spine.** It is already a first-class call to action on the site and the right choice. It is where these conversations actually convert, it feels personal, and it works across the timezone. Every funnel exit points to it.

**The nurture sequence (for the ones not ready today).** Most leads will not book this week. A structured sequence keeps BBDV present without nagging:

- Day 0: instant welcome plus their indicative quote and a clear next step.
- Day 1: the safety and surgeon credibility message (the number-one fear).
- Day 3: a real patient story that matches their procedure.
- Day 7: how the savings and EMI or 0% finance work, with a soft invite to a free consultation.
- Day 14, 30, 60: useful content, new stories, gentle check-ins, seasonal offers.

Delivered across email, WhatsApp, and SMS based on what each lead chose. This is automated, runs forever, and routinely recovers a meaningful share of "not now" leads into bookings months later. It is also where the website's content and the chatbot feed in.

## 7. Tracking, attribution, and the dashboard

If we cannot measure it, we are guessing. From day one:

- **Google Analytics 4 plus Google Ads conversion tracking:** every form start, form submit, WhatsApp click, and call counted, tied back to the keyword and ad that drove it.
- **Meta Pixel plus Conversions API:** so retargeting and lookalikes have clean data even with modern privacy limits.
- **Call and WhatsApp tracking:** know which ads produce real conversations, not just clicks.
- **A simple CRM or lead inbox:** every lead lands in one place with its score, source, and status (new, contacted, consult booked, deposit, booked, lost). This is the future dashboard already designed into the project shape.
- **A weekly one-page scorecard:** spend, leads, CPL, consults, bookings, CAC, ROAS, by channel. We make decisions off this, not vibes.

This is how A$3,000 a month becomes A$6,000 a month responsibly: we only scale what the numbers prove is working.

## 9. Compliance and risk (handled, not hidden)

Cosmetic surgery advertising is regulated, and we treat that as a feature, not a problem. It is also a competitive moat: the operators who ignore the rules get their ad accounts shut down and lose trust. We do it properly.

- **AHPRA and TGA (Australia):** no guaranteed outcomes, no trivialising surgery, careful and consented use of before-and-after, clear that results vary, and honest "indicative price, confirmed at consultation" language. BBDV positions as a coordination and facilitation service partnered with a board-certified clinic, which is exactly what it is.
- **Google and Meta healthcare policies:** ad copy and landing pages written to their cosmetic and health standards so accounts stay approved. Some claims and formats are restricted, and we work within them.
- **Trust and medical responsibility:** the chatbot and the copy never give medical advice or diagnosis. They inform and route to a real consultation. This is already built into the site and the assistant.

The net effect: an account that does not get banned, a brand patients trust with a serious decision, and a clean conscience.

## 10. The 90-day rollout and three budget scenarios

**Phase 1, weeks 1 to 2, foundation.** Connect the domain, go live, install all tracking (GA4, Google Ads, Meta Pixel and CAPI, call and WhatsApp tracking), build the first 3 to 5 landing pages, set up the lead inbox or CRM and the instant auto-responses, write the nurture sequence.

**Phase 2, weeks 3 to 6, ignite.** Launch Google Search on the top procedures plus brand and retargeting. Watch cost per lead daily, prune negatives, double down on the ad groups that convert. Refine landing pages from real behaviour.

**Phase 3, weeks 7 to 12, expand.** Add Meta prospecting and lookalikes, switch on YouTube, publish the first SEO comparison guides, broaden to more procedures, and scale the budget only on what the scorecard proves.

**Three budget scenarios (ad spend only, on top of the build and management):**

- **Starter, A$2,000 to A$3,000 / month.** Google Search on top procedures plus retargeting. Proves the model, expect 1 to 3 bookings a month rising.
- **Growth, A$4,000 to A$6,000 / month.** Search plus Meta plus full retargeting plus YouTube. Expect 4 to 8 bookings a month as it matures.
- **Scale, A$8,000+ / month.** All channels, multiple procedures, aggressive retargeting and lookalikes, SEO compounding underneath. Built only after the numbers justify it.

We start at Starter, prove the numbers, and let the results fund the climb. No leap of faith required.
